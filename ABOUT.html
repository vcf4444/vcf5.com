UNDERSTANDING CHITS

Chits, also known as chit funds or kuries, are a traditional form of collective savings and borrowing that have been prevalent in many cultures for centuries. In a chit fund, a group of individuals comes together to pool their money and contribute to a common fund. The fund is then utilized to provide loans or make investments, benefiting the participants. Chits are typically organized by chit fund companies, which manage the administrative aspects of the process.தமிழில் படிக்க மேல்நோக்கி ஸ்வைப் செய்யவும்

HOW CHITS WORK

Chits operate on a rotating basis, usually spanning a fixed duration, such as a month or a year. In each cycle, participants contribute a fixed amount of money to the chit fund, and one member is chosen through an auction or a lottery to receive the entire pool as a loan or an investment. 2.Disciplined saving: Chits encourage regular saving since participants are required to contribute a fixed amount periodically. This helps inculcate a savings habit and build financial discipline.

Benefits of Funds

1. Diversification: By investing in funds, individuals gain access to a diversified portfolio managed by professionals, reducing the risk associated with investing in individual securities.

2. Professional management: Funds are managed by experienced professionals who conduct in-depth research and analysis to make informed investment decisions on behalf of the investors.

3. Liquidity and transparency: Many funds offer high liquidity, allowing investors to buy or sell their shares on a daily basis. Additionally, funds provide regular reports and updates on their performance, ensuring transparency for investors.
